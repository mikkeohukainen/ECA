Cellular Automata

This website demonstrates Stephen Wolfram's principle of Elementary Cellular Automata.

The simulation consists of rows of cells (black/white squares), each row representing a new generation that evolves from the previous one following a simple set of rules.

A cell can have one of two states (0 or 1). The state of a cell in the next generation is determined by its current state and the state of its two immediate neighbors (the cell to its left and the cell to its right). At the start of the simulation (first row) only the middle cell has a value of 1.

Since there are 8 possible configurations for a group of three binary cells, the ruleset can be represented as an 8-bit binary number.

Read more on the official website. There you can also find illustrations of all 256 rules.
